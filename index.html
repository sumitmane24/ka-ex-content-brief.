Section 04
Do's & Don'ts
Follow these guidelines to create compliant, high-converting content
⚠️ Important: All health claims must be accurate and substantiated. Always add FDA disclaimer when using clinical stats. Pattern reserves the right to request revisions on non-compliant content before posting.
✓
DO'S
✓Show the mixing process — powder-to-drink or push-cap ASMR performs extremely well on TikTok
✓Emphasize "No Ashwagandha" — this is a proven hook topic on wellness TikTok
✓Show KA-EX on REST DAYS too — explain the 48-hour cortisol cycle and why daily use matters
✓Cite clinical data accurately: "up to 36% cortisol reduction in peer-reviewed studies" + disclaimer
✓Show product clearly on camera — full label visible for at least 3 seconds
✓Use smartwatch / HRV / sleep app screenshots to show personal data — hugely credible on TikTok
✓Mention Tudor Pro Cycling, Lara Gut-Behrami, or Fabian Cancellara for immediate credibility
✓Include #ad or #sponsored disclosure per FTC guidelines — in caption AND on-screen
✓Reference the Swiss Science story — ETH Zurich, 5 years research, 3 clinical trials, globally patented
✓Highlight "No caffeine" — caffeine actually raises cortisol, KA-EX is the anti-stress alternative
✗
DON'TS
✗Never claim KA-EX "cures," "treats," or "prevents" any disease or medical condition
✗Don't attack Ashwagandha competitor brands by name — frame it as a science comparison only
✗Don't exaggerate — say "up to 36% in clinical studies" not "will definitely reduce your cortisol by 36%"
✗Never create fake before/after transformations or fabricate personal testimonials
✗Don't skip the #ad / #paid disclosure — FTC compliance is required on every post
✗Avoid targeting content at users under 18 — KA-EX is an adult supplement
✗Don't post content with modified health claims without Pattern review first
✗Never state KA-EX replaces doctor-prescribed medication or mental health treatment
✗Don't edit out the FDA disclaimer when displaying clinical statistics on screen
✗Don't only show it post-workout — missing the "rest day" use case is leaving content opportunity on the table
